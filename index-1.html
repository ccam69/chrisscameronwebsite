Chris Cameron

I’m a scientist and researcher, interested in investigating human rights violations related to environmental injustices.

I am a scientist and researcher, investigating issues of human rights violations related to environmental injustices. As a legal investigator, I am researching existing humanitarian agreements and points of intervention that support strategic litigation for communities experiencing harm from data centers. As the program lead for the Data Centers and EJ program at Rooted Futures Lab, I am co-designing data visualisation tools, tracking data centers that are used for on-the-ground efforts done by journalists, activists, and community organizers. My background in digital storytelling, sonic investigations and environmental justice policy, has given me extensive experience translating investigative research into interactive tools and educational resources. I also host an investigative radio project called Sound Ecology, a space for climate-oriented artists to share their sonic toolkits for the climate collapse. Sound Ecology has been running since 2022 and we keep evolving, learning and moving into the investigation world.

I have a Master of Science degree from McGill University in Canada and I am fluent in English, French and Spanish. I am learning and speaking colloquial German.
